Chapter 3: Coordinate Geometry ‚Äì Notes

1. Introduction

Coordinate Geometry is the study of geometry using a coordinate system to represent points, lines, and figures on a plane.

2. Cartesian Plane

The Cartesian plane is formed by two perpendicular lines called axes: x-axis (horizontal) and y-axis (vertical) intersecting at the origin (0,0).

3. Coordinates of a Point

A point is represented by an ordered pair (x, y) where x is the horizontal distance from the y-axis and y is the vertical distance from the x-axis.

7. Collinear Points

Points are collinear if they lie on the same straight line. Check using slope or area method.

8. Slope of a Line

The slope of the line passing through points P(x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) and Q(x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ) is:

m = (y‚ÇÇ - y‚ÇÅ) / (x‚ÇÇ - x‚ÇÅ)

9. Equation of a Line

Equation of a line passing through point (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) with slope m:

y - y‚ÇÅ = m(x - x‚ÇÅ)

10. Plotting Points

Each solution of a linear equation is a point on the graph. Plot points (x, y) and draw a line passing through them.

11. Intersection of Lines

The solution of two linear equations in two variables is the point where their graphs intersect. Types of solutions:

  • Unique solution ‚Äì lines intersect at a point
  • No solution ‚Äì lines are parallel
  • Infinitely many solutions ‚Äì lines coincide
