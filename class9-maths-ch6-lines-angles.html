1. Introduction

Coordinate geometry deals with locating points in a plane using an ordered pair of numbers called coordinates (x, y).

2. Cartesian Plane

  • The plane is divided by two perpendicular axes: X-axis (horizontal) and Y-axis (vertical).
  • The point of intersection is called the origin (0,0).
  • Coordinates of a point: (x, y), where x is distance from Y-axis and y is distance from X-axis.

3. Plotting a Point

To plot a point (x, y):

  • Move x units along X-axis.
  • Move y units parallel to Y-axis.
  • Mark the point where it meets.

8. Slope of a Line

Slope of line joining (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) and (x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ):

Positive slope ‚Üí line rises, Negative slope ‚Üí line falls

9. Collinear Points

Three points are collinear if the slope between each pair is same.
